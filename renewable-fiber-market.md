<p><p>The renewable fiber market encompasses various types, including regenerated cellulose fiber, regenerated protein fiber, and Polynosic fibers. Regenerated cellulose fibers, derived from wood pulp or cotton, offer versatility and biodegradability. Regenerated protein fibers, made from natural proteins like soy or milk, provide unique properties such as softness and moisture-wicking capabilities. Polynosic fibers, a type of regenerated cellulose, enhance comfort and durability, making them ideal for apparel. Collectively, these fibers exemplify sustainable alternatives to traditional textiles.</p></p>
