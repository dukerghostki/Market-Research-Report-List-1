<p><p>The RF and microwave ceramic capacitors market is primarily divided into Class I and Class II types. Class I ceramic capacitors offer stable capacitance over varying temperatures and voltages, making them ideal for precision applications. In contrast, Class II ceramic capacitors provide higher capacitance values but with less stability and lower quality, suited for non-critical applications. Together, they serve a range of RF and microwave applications, ensuring performance in communication devices, filters, and resonators, catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>
