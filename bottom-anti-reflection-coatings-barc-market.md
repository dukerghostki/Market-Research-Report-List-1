<p><p>Bottom Anti-Reflection Coatings (BARC) are crucial in the semiconductor manufacturing process, minimizing reflections at the bottom of photoresist layers during lithography. The market is categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic BARC typically involves polymers that provide excellent performance in reducing reflectivity and improving feature resolution. In contrast, inorganic BARC consists of materials like silicon-based compounds, offering high stability and better adhesion. Both types are essential for enhancing the manufacturing efficiency and image quality in advanced photolithography techniques.</p></p>
